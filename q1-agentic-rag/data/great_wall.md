# The Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China is a series of fortifications built across the historical
northern borders of ancient Chinese states and Imperial China. It was constructed
to protect against raids and invasions from nomadic groups of the Eurasian Steppe.

## History

Construction of walls began as early as the 7th century BC, when individual states
built their own defences. These were later connected and extended under Qin Shi
Huang, the first Emperor of a unified China, around 220 BC. Most of the wall that
survives today was built during the Ming dynasty, between 1368 and 1644.

## Length and Construction

According to a comprehensive archaeological survey, the entire Great Wall, with all
of its branches, measures about 21,196 kilometres in total length. It was built
from a range of materials including rammed earth, wood, brick, and stone, depending
on the era and the resources available locally.

## Purpose Beyond Defence

Beyond defence, the wall served as a means of border control, allowing the
imposition of duties on goods transported along the Silk Road, regulating trade,
and controlling immigration and emigration.

## Modern Status

The Great Wall was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1987. It is one of
the most famous landmarks in the world and attracts millions of visitors each
year. Contrary to a popular myth, the wall is not generally visible to the naked
eye from space.
