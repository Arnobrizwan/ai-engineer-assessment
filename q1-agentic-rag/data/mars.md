# The Planet Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and the second-smallest planet in the
Solar System, after Mercury. It is often called the "Red Planet" because iron
oxide (rust) on its surface gives it a reddish appearance.

## Size and Orbit

Mars has a diameter of about 6,779 kilometres, roughly half that of Earth. It
orbits the Sun at an average distance of about 228 million kilometres and
completes one orbit every 687 Earth days. A day on Mars, called a sol, lasts
about 24 hours and 37 minutes.

## Moons

Mars has two small moons named Phobos and Deimos. Phobos is the larger and inner
moon, orbiting so close to Mars that it rises in the west and sets in the east.
Both moons are thought to be captured asteroids.

## Surface Features

Mars is home to Olympus Mons, the tallest volcano and one of the largest
mountains in the Solar System, standing about 22 kilometres high. It also hosts
Valles Marineris, a vast canyon system that stretches over 4,000 kilometres,
making it far longer than the Grand Canyon on Earth.

## Atmosphere

The Martian atmosphere is thin and composed mostly of carbon dioxide, about 95
percent. Surface temperatures are cold, averaging around minus 60 degrees
Celsius. Despite the harsh conditions, Mars remains a leading target in the
search for past or present microbial life.
